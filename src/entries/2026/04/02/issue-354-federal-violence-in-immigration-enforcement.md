On the morning of April 2, 2026, U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement officers attempted to arrest Ever Alvarenga Rios, a 32-year-old asylum seeker from Honduras, on the 200 block of South Haven Street in the Highlandtown neighborhood of Baltimore. According to Baltimore Police, the incident occurred at about 7:36 a.m. and involved a crash between two vehicles, one of them driven by a Department of Homeland Security official. Alvarenga Rios was hospitalized with what his attorneys described as significant injuries to his head, chest, back, and hands; two ICE officers were also taken to the hospital. He was then released from the hospital and transferred into ICE custody.

The fault narrative is sharply contested. Alvarenga Rios's attorneys and family say agents intentionally rear-ended his van as he drove to work and then used physical force against him; his wife, Lurbin Vasquez, and counsel say he has no criminal record, operates a flooring business, and has sought citizenship for eight years. DHS, through Acting Assistant Secretary Lauren Bis, said Alvarenga Rios "drove recklessly through the streets of Baltimore," caused a multi-car pileup, fled on foot, and that the two injured officers were expected to recover. Baltimore Police initially characterized both drivers' injuries as minor, a discrepancy with the hospital and attorney accounts that this record preserves rather than resolves. The abuse mapping rests on the use of coercive force in an immigration-enforcement operation, not on resolving who caused the collision.

After the arrest, attorneys for Alvarenga Rios said they were denied access to their client multiple times, both at the hospital and in custody, and a pastor was also turned away. Attorney Clarissa Lindsey said she was permitted only a brief conversation while third-party ICE contractors remained in the room, and that ICE told the contractor the client could not have legal counsel while hospitalized. Sen. Chris Van Hollen said the detention and denial of private attorney access amounted to "a clear denial of the due process rights afforded to all under our Constitution." The collision and use of force map to violence in immigration enforcement; the repeated refusal of attorney and clergy access maps to denial of counsel.
