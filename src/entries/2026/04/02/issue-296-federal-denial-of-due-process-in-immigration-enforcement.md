On April 2, 2026, U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement officers detained Annie Ramos, a 22-year-old college student, at Fort Polk (now Fort Johnson) in Vernon Parish, Louisiana. Ramos had traveled to the base with her husband, Army Staff Sgt. Matthew Blank, whom she married in March, to begin the process of registering as a military spouse, activating benefits, and taking steps toward a green card. Brought to the United States from Honduras before she was two years old, Ramos was taken into custody on a final order of removal that an immigration judge had entered in 2005, after her family failed to appear at a hearing when she was an infant.

The due-process concern is that the order underpinning the detention was issued against Ramos in absentia in 2005, when she was younger than two and could not have participated in her own proceeding. ICE executed that 21-year-old order while she voluntarily appeared to regularize her status, with a 2020 DACA application that her husband says has remained "in limbo" amid litigation over the program. The Department of Homeland Security said Ramos "has no legal status to be in this country" and that "this administration is not going to ignore the rule of law."

Ramos was released after about five days in detention on an order of supervision with a GPS ankle monitor and weekly ICE check-ins, her removal case still pending. The arrest of a service member's spouse on a military installation drew backlash from military-family advocates, who noted that DHS in 2025 eliminated a 2022 policy treating a relative's military service as a "significant mitigating factor" in enforcement decisions. Her release does not erase the detention as a recorded event.
