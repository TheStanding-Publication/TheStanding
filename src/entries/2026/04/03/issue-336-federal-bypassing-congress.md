On April 3, 2026, President Donald J. Trump issued a presidential memorandum,
"Liberating the Department of Homeland Security From the Democrat-Caused
Shutdown," addressed to the Secretary of Homeland Security and the Director of
the Office of Management and Budget. The memorandum states that nearly seven
weeks had elapsed since the lapse in DHS appropriations began on February 14,
2026, and that more than 35,000 employees — including Coast Guard civilians,
FEMA personnel, and cybersecurity staff at CISA — had gone roughly two months
without pay. On that basis the President determined that "these circumstances
constitute an emergency situation compromising the Nation's security" and
directed the Secretary, in coordination with the OMB Director, to "use funds
that have a reasonable and logical nexus to the functions of DHS" to provide
every DHS employee the compensation and benefits that would have accrued had
the shutdown not occurred, "consistent with applicable law, including 31
U.S.C. 1301(a)."

The constitutional question is the power of the purse. Spending in the absence
of an appropriation is the province of Congress, and the Antideficiency Act
(31 U.S.C. 1341) generally bars obligating funds during a lapse. By
unilaterally declaring an emergency and directing DHS and OMB to repurpose
funds to pay employees, the executive substitutes its own determination for
the appropriation Congress had not enacted — the substance of bypassing
Congress, where Congress is the proper actor, and of executive overreach
straining the statutory limits the memorandum itself invokes. The memorandum
does not identify the specific accounts to be tapped or the precise legal
mechanism for moving the money, and contemporaneous reporting noted that
omission.

The memorandum hedges that it is "subject to the availability of
appropriations" and that it creates no rights enforceable at law, but its
operative directive — pay all employees now — is concrete and was reported as
taking effect. It followed a narrower March 27, 2026 memorandum that directed
pay for Transportation Security Administration officers and employees; that
earlier action is related but distinct (different date, narrower scope) and is
not currently recorded in the archive. The DHS appropriations lapse that
prompted the memorandum had begun February 14, 2026.
