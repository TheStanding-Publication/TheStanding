On April 28, 2026, a federal grand jury in the Eastern District of North Carolina returned a two-count indictment against former FBI Director James Comey, 65, charging him with threatening the life of President Donald J. Trump in violation of 18 U.S.C. § 871 and transmitting a threat in interstate commerce in violation of 18 U.S.C. § 875(c). Each count carries a maximum penalty of 10 years in prison. The charges rest entirely on a May 15, 2025 Instagram photograph of seashells arranged to read "86 47" — which the government contends a reasonable recipient familiar with the circumstances would interpret as a serious expression of intent to harm the 47th president. Acting Attorney General Todd Blanche framed the case as enforcing the law against political violence, saying that "anyone who dials it up and threatens the life of the President will be held accountable"; the announcement was made by U.S. Attorney W. Ellis Boyle, whose office is prosecuting the case, and FBI Director Kash Patel said Comey "disgracefully encouraged a threat on President Trump's life."

The indictment is the Justice Department's second criminal case against Comey, one of President Trump's most prominent critics, after a federal judge dismissed an earlier false-statements and obstruction indictment in November 2025. The charged conduct is itself a since-deleted social-media post, placing the prosecution squarely at the intersection of selective prosecution, the weaponization of the Justice Department, and the prosecution of protected speech. The DOJ's own release reminds that "an indictment is merely an accusation" and that Comey is presumed innocent; Comey has publicly maintained he is "still innocent" and "still not afraid."

Reporting that the "seashells" matter sat dormant until Attorney General Pam Bondi was fired — reportedly for insufficiently aggressive prosecution of the president's foes — and was then revived under Acting AG Blanche bears directly on the political-alignment element of these abuses. The pattern echoes Blanche's April 7, 2026 statement that the president has a "right" and "duty" to order DOJ to investigate his enemies (recorded separately in the archive): same actor, same abuse pattern, a distinct event. This entry is anchored to the April 28 indictment itself; the earlier dismissed indictment and the surrounding personnel reporting are related context, not the charged event.
