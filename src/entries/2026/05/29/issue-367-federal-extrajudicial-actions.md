On May 29, 2026, U.S. Southern Command announced it had struck a
vessel in the eastern Pacific Ocean that it described as "engaged in
narco-trafficking operations," killing three men. SOUTHCOM said
intelligence had confirmed the boat was "transiting along known
narco-trafficking routes," that the strike was carried out at the
direction of Gen. Francis L. Donovan — the top U.S. commander in Latin
America — and that no U.S. troops were harmed. The command released a
roughly 15-second video of the strike, this time in color rather than
the black-and-white footage that had accompanied earlier
announcements. It was at least the third lethal strike that week in
the eastern Pacific; with it, the open-ended military campaign begun
in early September has now killed at least 202 people across the
eastern Pacific and Caribbean theaters.

As with prior strikes in the campaign, the Pentagon published no
evidence that the vessel was carrying drugs, and those aboard were
afforded no arrest, charge, or trial — the basis on which Democratic
lawmakers, military lawyers, and outside legal scholars have argued
the strikes amount to extrajudicial killings of criminal suspects
outside any congressionally authorized armed conflict. Congress has
not authorized hostilities against Latin American drug-trafficking
organizations; President Trump has publicly characterized the campaign
as an "armed conflict" with the cartels. The campaign's use of
uniformed armed forces to carry out what functions as a lethal
drug-enforcement program — announced with a recurring stock
characterization while no supporting evidence is released — is the
basis for the politicization-of-uniformed-services mapping, consistent
with the archive's treatment of the earlier strikes in this series.

The strike is part of Operation Southern Spear, the Pentagon's name
for its campaign against alleged drug trafficking in Latin American
waters, which has increased in frequency in recent weeks. The
Department of Defense's Inspector General announced the week before
this strike that it would self-initiate a review of whether the
strikes followed the military's established targeting guidelines; the
review was not prompted by a congressional query. Reporting on the
surrounding days was inconsistent on the exact tally and sequencing —
some outlets described a later, distinct strike on May 30 as the
"fourth attack of the week" that brought the cumulative toll to 205 —
but the dated Stars and Stripes account, Newsweek, and U.S. News place
this color-video strike, directed by Gen. Donovan, on Friday, May 29,
2026, as the third attack of the week, with a cumulative campaign toll
of roughly 202.
