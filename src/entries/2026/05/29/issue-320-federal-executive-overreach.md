On May 29, 2026 — John F. Kennedy's birthday — U.S. District Judge Christopher
Cooper found that the Trump-appointed Kennedy Center board lacked the authority
to rename the federally chartered institution on its own and ordered the
administration to strip President Donald Trump's name from the building and the
Center's website within 14 days. The board had rechristened the venue the
"Trump Kennedy Center," lettering on the building reading "The Donald J. Trump
and John F. Kennedy Memorial Center for the Performing Arts." Cooper rejected
the administration's argument that this was a mere "secondary name," writing
that the Center's organic statute "makes crystal clear that the Center is to be
named for President Kennedy" and that "Congress gave the Kennedy Center its
name, and only Congress can change it."

The same ruling issued a preliminary injunction halting the board's plan to
close the Center for roughly two years for renovations, a closure the board had
approved at a March 2026 vote after Trump — who had installed himself as board
chair the prior year — called for it. Cooper described the closure vote as an
"ill-informed and seemingly preordained decision" that rested on "an
insufficient, one-sided presentation of information," while making clear the
needed repair work could still proceed and that the board could revisit closure
through a proper process. The court also ordered that Rep. Joyce Beatty
(D-Ohio), an ex officio trustee whose lawsuit prompted the ruling and who had
been stripped of her board voting rights, have those rights restored.

This entry records the executive branch's override of a congressional statute,
not the court's adjudication of it. The renaming and the unilateral closure
were acts the board took at Trump's direction that exceeded its statutory
authority (`executive-overreach`) and substituted board fiat for the
congressional actor the law designates (`bypassing-congress`); the May 29
ruling is the event that placed that sequence in the public record as unlawful.
The court's separate finding on Beatty's voting rights touches legislative
independence but is left untagged here, as the record does not establish the
removal as retaliation for her votes or dissent. Trump responded to the ruling
on Truth Social by saying he had "no interest" in continuing the overhaul and
would work with Congress to transfer the institution back to lawmakers; the
Kennedy Center signaled it would appeal.
