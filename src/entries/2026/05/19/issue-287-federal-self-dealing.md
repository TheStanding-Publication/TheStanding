David Rush, a senior executive service-level officer at the Central Intelligence Agency, was arrested on May 19, 2026 and charged with a single felony count of theft of public money. According to an FBI affidavit filed May 20, agents who searched Rush's Virginia home the day before his arrest seized roughly 303 one-kilogram gold bars valued at more than $40 million, about $2 million in U.S. currency, and some 35 luxury watches, many of them Rolexes. The case originated inside the CIA: an internal investigation flagged potential legal violations, and agency director John Ratcliffe referred the matter to the FBI.

Prosecutors allege that between November 2025 and March 2026 Rush requested and received "a significant quantity of foreign currency and tens of millions of dollars in gold bars for work-related expenses" from the government, but that the CIA was later unable to locate the gold or much of the currency, or any record of how Rush had disposed of it. The affidavit further alleges he sustained his career through fabricated credentials — claiming degrees from Clemson University and Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute and service as a Navy pilot — none of which investigators could substantiate, and that he claimed roughly $77,000 in military leave compensation for Navy Reserve service that records do not support after his 2015 honorable discharge.

The central abuse here is self-dealing: a senior official allegedly using his official requisitioning authority to convert public assets to personal enrichment, with monetizing-office as a secondary characterization of the same conduct. Notably, the oversight mechanism functioned in this instance, with the CIA's own internal review surfacing the conduct and triggering the referral rather than external whistleblowing. The charges are allegations that remain to be tested in court; Rush was held pending a detention hearing in federal court in Alexandria, Virginia. Some downstream reporting has noted alleged professional ties between Rush and a senior administration official; that thread is unconfirmed and is not part of the documented abuse.
