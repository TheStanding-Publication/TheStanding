Election denial is the public assertion by officeholders, candidates, or party officials that a legitimate election was stolen, rigged, or otherwise illegitimate, in the absence of evidence sufficient to support the claim. It is distinct from challenging specific irregularities through legal channels, which is routine; the issue is the assertion itself, made to constituencies who treat the speaker's word as evidence. Election denial corrodes the bargain elections depend on — that losers accept results — and supplies the rhetorical foundation for [refusal to certify](/ideals/free-and-fair-elections/certification-refusal/), [alternate-elector schemes](/ideals/free-and-fair-elections/alternate-electors/), and [post-election overturning attempts](/ideals/free-and-fair-elections/post-election-overturning-attempts/). The publication records denial that originates from officials and candidates, not denial in private speech or social-media noise.
