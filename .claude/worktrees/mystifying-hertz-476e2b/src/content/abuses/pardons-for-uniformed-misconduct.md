Pardons or clemency for service members convicted of war crimes, serious misconduct, or unlawful violence — granted for political effect rather than after the deliberate processes that military justice and clemency systems provide — undercut both civilian control and the integrity of military discipline. The publication tracks cases where the clemency action bypasses or overrides the recommendations of military leadership and the trial record, regardless of which administration grants them.
